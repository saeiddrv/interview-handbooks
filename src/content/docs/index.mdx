## Why this exists

These handbooks lean toward the **backend and the Java & Kotlin ecosystem** — the JVM, Spring,
persistence, messaging, and the distributed systems built on top of them — alongside the
architecture, operations, and people skills that senior+ rounds actually test.

Even in the AI era, the fundamentals still decide who gets the offer. AI can generate code, but it
can't replace an engineer who **understands why** — who knows how a `HashMap` resizes, why a queue
beats a synchronous call, what an error budget protects, and how to reason about consistency under
failure. Tools change; the principles you're hired to apply do not. This library is about knowing
them deeply enough to explain, defend, and act on them — in an interview and on the job.

The handbooks are **written from real engineering experience and refined, fact-checked, and
consistency-edited with the help of AI** — so explanations stay clear, examples stay correct, and the
house style stays uniform across the whole library.
